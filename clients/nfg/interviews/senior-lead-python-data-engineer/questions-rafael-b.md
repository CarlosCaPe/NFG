# Interview Questions — Rafael B.

**Position**: Senior/Lead Python Data Engineer
**Tone**: Friendly, conversational — get to know the person, not a quiz.
**Tip**: Let them talk. Follow up on what sounds interesting. These are conversation starters, not a checklist.

---

## 1. The Migration Story
"Rafael, I see you led a migration from R and Java to Python between 2019 and 2024. That's a big shift for a team. What was the hardest part about convincing people to move away from the tools they already knew?"

🇪🇸 "Rafael, veo que lideraste una migración de R y Java a Python entre 2019 y 2024. Es un cambio grande para un equipo. ¿Qué fue lo más difícil de convencer a la gente de dejar las herramientas que ya conocían?"

> **Why ask**: Tests leadership soft skills, change management. The role requires defining engineering standards — he did this already.

## 2. Airflow in Practice
"You built custom Airflow sensors and operators across two different jobs. If you had to explain to someone non-technical what an Airflow sensor does and why it matters, how would you describe it?"

🇪🇸 "Construiste sensores y operadores custom de Airflow en dos trabajos diferentes. Si tuvieras que explicarle a alguien no técnico qué hace un sensor de Airflow y por qué importa, ¿cómo lo describirías?"

> **Why ask**: Tests communication skill + depth of understanding. The role uses Dagster (similar orchestrator) — his Airflow experience maps directly.

## 3. The Oracle Linux Chapter
"Your most recent role was deep into Oracle Linux, RPM packages, and CVE patching. That's a pretty different world from data pipelines. What drew you to that role, and what did you bring back from it?"

🇪🇸 "Tu rol más reciente fue bien metido en Oracle Linux, paquetes RPM y parches de CVE. Es un mundo bastante diferente a los data pipelines. ¿Qué te atrajo de ese rol, y qué te trajiste de vuelta?"

> **Why ask**: Understand career trajectory. This role was less data-engineering and more infra — want to hear how he connects the dots.

## 4. Teaching & Leading
"You mention leading internal courses and training on open-source tools multiple times. What was the best course you taught, and how did you know it actually landed with the team?"

🇪🇸 "Mencionas varias veces que lideraste cursos internos y capacitaciones en herramientas open-source. ¿Cuál fue el mejor curso que diste, y cómo supiste que realmente le sirvió al equipo?"

> **Why ask**: The role requires mentoring senior engineers. His teaching background is a strong signal — let him show it.

## 5. The Freight Forecasting Model
"You implemented forecasting models for freight prices. That sounds like it touches both data engineering and data science. How did you approach that, and did you own the model end-to-end or was there a data science team involved?"

🇪🇸 "Implementaste modelos de forecasting para precios de flete. Eso suena a que toca tanto data engineering como data science. ¿Cómo lo abordaste? ¿Eras dueño del modelo de punta a punta o había un equipo de data science involucrado?"

> **Why ask**: Understand boundaries — the role is data engineering, not data science. Does he know where one ends and the other begins?

## 6. Dagster & Snowflake Gap
"The position works daily with Dagster and Snowflake. I see you have Airflow and Snowflake on your CV, but not Dagster specifically. Have you looked into it at all? What do you think the learning curve would be coming from Airflow?"

🇪🇸 "La posición trabaja diario con Dagster y Snowflake. Veo que tienes Airflow y Snowflake en tu CV, pero no Dagster específicamente. ¿Le has echado un ojo? ¿Cómo crees que sería la curva de aprendizaje viniendo de Airflow?"

> **Why ask**: Direct gap check, but framed positively. Airflow→Dagster is very transferable — want to hear self-awareness.

## 7. Healthcare & Compliance
"This project is in healthcare, with HIPAA and regulated data. Have you worked in an environment where you had to think about data sensitivity — who can see what, audit trails, that kind of thing?"

🇪🇸 "Este proyecto es en healthcare, con HIPAA y datos regulados. ¿Has trabajado en un ambiente donde tenías que pensar en la sensibilidad de los datos — quién puede ver qué, audit trails, ese tipo de cosas?"

> **Why ask**: No healthcare experience on CV. The role requires it. Want to know if he's worked in ANY regulated environment.

## 8. The Neural Network Side Project
"I noticed you built neural networks for product categorization on Linio, Mercadolibre, and Amazon. That sounds like a fun challenge. Was that a production system or more of a research/prototype thing?"

🇪🇸 "Vi que construiste redes neuronales para categorización de productos en Linio, Mercadolibre y Amazon. Suena como un reto divertido. ¿Eso fue un sistema en producción o más un prototipo/investigación?"

> **Why ask**: Shows curiosity and depth. Also tests honesty — was it real production ML or a POC that never shipped?

## 9. CI/CD Philosophy
"You migrated unit tests from Jenkins to GitHub Actions. What made you push for that move, and how did the team respond?"

🇪🇸 "Migraste los unit tests de Jenkins a GitHub Actions. ¿Qué te motivó a impulsar ese cambio, y cómo respondió el equipo?"

> **Why ask**: The role leads CI/CD standardization with GitHub Actions. He's done this exact migration. Let him tell the story.

## 10. What Excites You About This Role
"Looking at this position — Python-heavy data platform, Dagster, Snowflake, healthcare domain, team standardization work — what part of it gets you most excited, and what part makes you a little nervous?"

🇪🇸 "Viendo esta posición — plataforma de datos heavy en Python, Dagster, Snowflake, dominio healthcare, estandarización de equipo — ¿qué parte te emociona más, y qué parte te pone un poco nervioso?"

> **Why ask**: Open-ended closer. Shows self-awareness, motivation, and honesty. The "nervous" part reveals where they'd need ramp-up.

---

**Scoring Notes** (for your reference during the call):

| Signal | Strong | Weak |
|--------|--------|------|
| Airflow→Dagster readiness | "I've looked at Dagster, the asset-based model is different but..." | "I don't know what Dagster is" |
| Leadership | Specific stories with team outcomes | "I managed people" (vague) |
| Healthcare awareness | Any regulated env experience | No awareness of data sensitivity |
| Communication | Clear, structured answers | Rambling, can't simplify |
| Self-awareness | Honest about gaps | Over-claims or dodges |
